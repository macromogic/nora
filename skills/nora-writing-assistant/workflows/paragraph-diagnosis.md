# Workflow: Paragraph Diagnosis

Use this workflow when a paragraph "isn't working" but it's not clear why — diagnose before rewriting.

## Goal

A clear, specific diagnosis of what's structurally wrong with a paragraph (or a few), and a recommendation for which workflow to use next — not necessarily a rewrite itself.

## Procedure

### 1. Read the paragraph(s) in isolation and in context

If the paragraph is part of a larger section, check the paragraphs immediately before/after it — many paragraph-level problems are actually transition or ordering problems.

### 2. Check for common structural issues

- **Unclear or missing topic sentence**: does the first sentence tell the reader what the paragraph is about?
- **Buried claim**: is the actual point of the paragraph stated late, or only implied?
- **Mixed ideas**: does the paragraph try to make more than one point that would be clearer split apart?
- **Unsupported jump in logic**: does a sentence assume a connection the reader hasn't been given yet?
- **Orphaned citation**: is a citation present without a clear claim it's attached to, or a claim present without support where one seems expected?
- **Weak transitions**: does the paragraph connect awkwardly to what comes before/after?

### 3. Diagnose, don't rewrite

State which issue(s) apply, with specifics (quote the relevant sentence(s)). Do not produce a full rewrite as part of this workflow — that's `polish` (wording-level fix) or `restructure` (structural fix).

### 4. Output

End with the diagnosis and a recommendation: which workflow (`polish` or `restructure`) would address it, and roughly what that would involve. If the issue is actually about claim strength or citation support rather than structure, recommend `overclaim-check` instead.
